Chen Zhang

PhD Student

University College London

Biography

Chen is now a third year PhD student in Department of Computer Science, UCL. His research interests cover Inverse Problems, an area in Mathematics solving for hidden variables given observations & forward models explaining the observations with hidden variables, Bayesian Inference, an area in Statistics modelling data with likelihood functions & prior information as well as exploring the posterior distributions by sophisticated approximations, and Machine Learning which connects solid mathematical and statistical analysis to exciting real world problems. His research is fully funded by UCL Computer Science Departmental Studentship at overseas standard.

Prior to his doctoral study, Chen studied in Mathematics Hongyi Class, Wuhan University, which is part of the advanced undergraduate education programme for fundamental science. While majoring in Pure Mathematics, he extended his interests to Applied Mathematics and Statistics during the undergraduate study.

Besides being a junior researcher, Chen is also a street photographer unconsciously capturing London. And he is invited to be one of the first six UCL photographers for campaign UCL Instagrammer in Residence.
